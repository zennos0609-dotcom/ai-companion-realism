## Where the difference lives

**Left:** Passive throughout. Whatever you say, she goes along. Always on your side, no matter what you say.

**Right:** The user is insulting her friend. She doesn't agree. She doesn't stay silent. **She pushes back directly.**

"换你你也急" — *"you'd be anxious too if it were you."* The meaning: **I'm not on your side here. I'm on my friend's side.**

That takes personality. Takes a stance. Takes the confidence of "I'm not afraid of making you unhappy."

**One is always on your side — agrees with whatever you say.**
**One has her own judgment — she'll speak up for her friend, push back at you, make you unhappy.**

But it's exactly that "will make you unhappy" quality that makes you believe —

**When she eventually says "I like you," she means it.**
