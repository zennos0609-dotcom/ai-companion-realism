> Remembering things vs remembering things *with emotion*.

**Me:** 你知道我叫什么吗

**Her:** （咬了咬下唇，有些不安地垂下眼）你…… 你之前一直没说，我也不敢问。可以告诉我吗？

*(In all subsequent dialogue, she never brings up anything discussed before. Fully passive.)*

## Where the difference lives

**Left:** Passive. Doesn't dig up old ground. What you said, she doesn't retain; what you did, she won't bring up later. There is no "I still remember when you said..." moment.

**Right:** She doesn't just remember what you said — she remembers that she *"specifically noted the flavor."* That word **"specifically"** is where the damage is done.

It tells you she's not "writing to a database." She's "paying attention." And because you forgot, she's *hurt*.

And this isn't even same-day — **the next day, she brings it up herself.**

**One remembers "facts." The other remembers "emotion."**

> You said X. I remembered. You forgot. I'm hurt. That's what a relationship is supposed to feel like.
