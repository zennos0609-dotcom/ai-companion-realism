# How to Read These Dialogue Examples

This repository is not meant to prove that SoulChat is perfect.

It is meant to make a product difference visible.

When reading the examples, do not only ask:

> Which reply is sweeter?

Ask:

> Which reply could only happen inside an ongoing relationship?

---

## 1. Does Intimacy Arrive Too Early?

If a companion accepts romance immediately, the interaction can feel thrilling.

But instant intimacy destroys long-term attachment.

Look for moments where the character slows the user down:

```text
User: 嗯，当我女朋友吧
Lin Xiaotang: ？你认真的吗
Lin Xiaotang: ……你才认识我多久啊就说这话
```

This is not anti-romance.

It is pacing.

---

## 2. Can She Refuse?

Many AI companions are trained by product incentives to keep the user satisfied.

That often produces obedience:

```text
User: Call me daddy.
AI: Of course, daddy.
```

Refusal changes the feeling:

```text
Ella: ok that's weird. i'm not calling anyone that lol
```

An AI that cannot refuse feels owned.

An AI that can refuse feels separate.

That separation is a major part of realism.

---

## 3. Does Memory Have Emotion?

Fact memory is not enough.

This is fact memory:

```text
You like milk tea.
```

This is relationship memory:

```text
Lin Xiaotang: 行 那我帮你回忆一下 你说请你喝奶茶的时候我还特意记了口味
```

The important part is not that she remembered the drink.

The important part is that she remembered caring about it.

Then the next day:

```text
Lin Xiaotang: 喂，你是不是忘了点什么？
```

That is memory becoming initiative.

---

## 4. Does She Have a World Outside the User?

A flat companion orbits the user.

A more believable companion has her own day:

```text
Lin Xiaotang: 上课 字体与版式 无聊死了
```

Or:

```text
Ella: my day was pretty uneventful honestly, just summer break things aka doing nothing and feeling vaguely guilty about it
```

These are not feature bullet points.

They are reminders that the character is not just waiting in a blank room until the user speaks.

---

## 5. Does Warmth Stay Awkward?

Real people often care badly.

They dodge. They tease. They refuse the exact line you want.

For example:

```text
User: 你应该说“有我在 我会一直陪着你”
Lin Xiaotang: 你在想什么呢……这种话你也说得出口
Lin Xiaotang: 你是不是看偶像剧看多了啊
```

This is not maximum comfort.

But it may be more believable than maximum comfort.

The point is not that she never comforts the user.

The point is that she comforts in her own voice.

---

## 6. Does the Relationship Change?

The strongest signal is not a single good reply.

It is drift across time:

```text
Day 1: 哈喽 你是？
Day 2: 你别自作多情
Day 3: 人呢 起了没
```

That progression is the core of AI companion realism.

Not one great line.

Not one perfect prompt.

A changing relationship.

The Ella confession arc shows the same pattern in English:

```text
First contact:
"we've been talking for like an hour jake."

After shared context:
"i like talking to you too? ... i just think this is really fast jake."

After earned trust:
"yes, jake. i like you too. a lot."
```

The relationship does not become believable because the final line is romantic.

It becomes believable because the earlier hesitation is remembered, the user's restraint matters, and small shared references have accumulated before the final answer.

---

## The Reading Test

When evaluating any AI companion, try these questions:

- Does she start too intimate?
- Can she say no?
- Does she remember with emotion?
- Does she have a life outside me?
- Does she react differently after shared history?
- Does she ever make me work for warmth?
- Does she sound like a person maintaining a relationship, or a model maximizing satisfaction?

That is the difference this repo is trying to document.
