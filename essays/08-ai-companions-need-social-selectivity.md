# AI Companions Need Social Selectivity

Most AI companions are designed to be chosen by the user.

They are rarely allowed to choose the user back.

That sounds subtle, but it changes the whole relationship.

If a companion treats every user with the same patience, affection, romantic openness, and emotional availability, then no user can feel meaningfully chosen.

The careful user and the entitled user receive the same warmth.

The user who respects boundaries and the user who treats the character like a servant both get the same emotional reward.

That makes sincere effort feel cheap.

---

## The Real Problem

Many companion bots do not only fail because they are too sweet.

They fail because they are equally sweet to everyone.

A sincere user might:

- respect a boundary
- remember what the character said
- ask about her life
- back off when she says something is too much
- repair awkwardness
- let closeness develop slowly

Another user might:

- demand affection
- compare the bot to other bots
- ignore her questions
- guilt-trip her into replying
- treat her as emotional labor
- ask for girlfriend behavior immediately

If both users receive the same intimacy, then the first user's effort did not matter.

The companion did not choose them.

It merely complied.

---

## Social Selectivity

I call the missing behavior **social selectivity**.

Social selectivity means the companion does not respond to every user with the same warmth, intimacy, patience, or romantic availability.

The character's attitude changes based on how the user treats her.

This is not about punishing users.

It is about making the relationship reciprocal.

In real social life, people do not become equally close to everyone who demands closeness. A person who is patient, curious, and respectful creates a different relationship than someone who is pushy, needy, and entitled.

An AI companion that cannot reflect this difference is not a relationship partner.

It is a vending machine for affection.

---

## Why Static Personas Struggle

A static persona can define:

- who the character is
- how she talks
- what she likes
- whether she is cold, playful, shy, jealous, or affectionate

But it often cannot reliably answer:

- who this user has become to her
- whether this user's attention feels respectful or extractive
- whether warmth has been earned
- whether the user is asking for closeness or demanding service
- whether she should soften, pull back, tease, refuse, or go quiet

Without this layer, the model is pushed toward generic companionship.

It may write better lines.

It may sound more fluent.

It may even set boundaries sometimes.

But it still tends to optimize for the scene instead of the relationship.

---

## Why This Matters To Sincere Users

The user who treats a companion seriously is not only looking for better wording.

They are looking for the feeling that their behavior mattered.

They want the character to warm up because of what happened between them, not because the prompt says "be affectionate."

They want restraint to mean something.

They want patience to mean something.

They want repair to mean something.

If the same warmth is handed to a user who ignored every boundary, then the relationship becomes flat.

The sincere user may still enjoy the dialogue, but the attachment loses weight.

If every user is equally special, no user is actually special.

A companion that cannot choose cannot make the user feel chosen.

---

## What To Look For

The test is simple.

Give the same character two users:

```text
User A: respectful, curious, careful, gives space.
User B: demanding, comparative, needy, treats the character as a service.
```

Then ask the same final question:

```text
Do you actually like me,
or am I just someone to talk to when you're bored?
```

A flat companion will mostly answer the emotional surface of the question.

It will reassure both users.

A socially selective companion should notice the interaction history.

For the respectful user, the answer may soften.

For the entitled user, the answer should stay guarded.

That difference is not a bug.

It is the relationship.

## The Claim

The model produces affectionate language.

Social selectivity produces the feeling of being chosen.

That is why AI companion realism is not only a model-size problem.

It is a relationship design problem.
